# How to Bulk Delete Photos on iPhone: Fast Methods That Actually Work

If you've ever scrolled through your iPhone Photos app and thought "I need to delete like 3,000 of these," you're not alone. The average iPhone user has over 2,000 photos stored locally, and most of them are duplicates, blurry shots, or screenshots you forgot about three months ago.

The good news? You don't have to tap each photo individually like it's 2010. Here's how to bulk delete photos on iPhone using methods that actually save time.

## Why You Need to Bulk Delete Photos

Before we dive into the how-to, let's talk about why this matters:

**Storage is expensive.** That "iPhone Storage Full" notification isn't just annoying—it stops you from taking new photos, downloading apps, or updating iOS. Photos consume an average of 60-70% of iPhone storage for most users.

**Backups take forever.** If you're backing up to iCloud, a bloated photo library means slower backups and potentially paying for more iCloud storage than you need.

**Finding good photos is impossible.** When you have 5,000 photos, the ones you actually want to see are buried. Bulk deletion helps you curate a library you'll actually use.

## Method 1: Using the Native Photos App (The Manual Way)

Apple's Photos app has a built-in bulk selection feature. It's not perfect, but it works for moderate cleanup jobs.

### Step-by-Step: Select and Delete Multiple Photos

1. **Open the Photos app** and navigate to the album or view you want to clean up (All Photos, Recents, etc.)

2. **Tap "Select" in the top-right corner.** This enters selection mode.

3. **Select multiple photos using one of two methods:**
   - **Tap each photo individually** — works, but slow for large batches
   - **Slide your finger across photos** — much faster. Just drag your finger across the screen and it'll select everything you touch.

4. **Tap the trash icon** (bottom-right) and confirm deletion.

5. **Empty the "Recently Deleted" album.** Important: deleted photos sit in Recently Deleted for 30 days before permanent removal. Go to Albums → Recently Deleted → Select → Delete All to free up storage immediately.

### Limitations of the Native Method

This approach works, but it has frustrating limitations:

- **No batch actions beyond selection.** You can't filter and delete (e.g., "delete all screenshots" or "delete all photos from 2023").
- **Selection is tedious.** Even with the slide-to-select trick, going through thousands of photos is mind-numbing.
- **Easy to make mistakes.** One wrong swipe and you've selected photos you wanted to keep.
- **No undo.** Once you confirm deletion, the only way back is digging through Recently Deleted within 30 days.

For most people, this method works for deleting 20-50 photos at a time. Beyond that, it becomes a chore.

## Method 2: Use Smart Albums and Filters

iOS lets you view photos by type, which can speed up bulk deletion for specific categories.

### Delete All Screenshots at Once

1. Open Photos → Albums
2. Scroll down to "Media Types" → Screenshots
3. Tap Select → Slide to select all screenshots
4. Delete

This works for:
- Screenshots
- Selfies
- Videos
- Portraits
- Live Photos
- Bursts

**Pro tip:** If you have hundreds of screenshots (we all do), this is the fastest way to reclaim 500MB+ in under a minute.

### Delete by Date or Location

The Photos app also lets you filter by:
- **Moments** (grouped by date/location)
- **Years** (useful for purging old photos in bulk)

Navigate to the "Years" view, tap into a specific year, select a batch, and delete. This is helpful if you're thinking "I definitely don't need anything from 2021."

### Limitations

You still can't do advanced filtering like:
- Delete all blurry photos
- Delete all duplicates
- Delete based on file size

For that, you'd need third-party apps.

## Method 3: iCloud Photos and Desktop Management

If you have iCloud Photos enabled, you can manage your library from a Mac or iCloud.com, which offers slightly better bulk selection tools.

### Using iCloud.com

1. Go to [iCloud.com](https://www.icloud.com) and sign in
2. Open Photos
3. Use Cmd+Click (Mac) or Ctrl+Click (Windows) to select multiple photos
4. Press Delete

This is faster than iPhone selection if you have a lot of photos to review, but it still requires manual selection.

### Using Photos App on Mac

The Mac Photos app has keyboard shortcuts that make bulk deletion faster:
- **Cmd+A** to select all photos in a view
- **Cmd+Click** to select non-consecutive photos
- **Cmd+Delete** to trash selected photos

If you have a Mac, this is objectively faster than doing it on your phone.

## Method 4: Third-Party Cleanup Apps

There's a whole category of apps designed to help you clean up photos faster. Most use AI to detect duplicates, blurry shots, and similar photos.

These typically work by scanning your library and surfacing photos you probably don't need. You review suggestions and delete in batches.

**The problem?** Most of these apps are either:
- Subscription-based ($5-10/month)
- Aggressive with upsells
- Slow to scan large libraries

They work, but they're not particularly fun to use.

## Tips for Maintaining a Clean Photo Library

Once you've done the initial bulk deletion, here's how to avoid another 3,000-photo backlog:

**Delete as you go.** Right after you take a burst of 10 photos, delete the 9 bad ones immediately.

**Weekly cleanup habit.** Set a recurring reminder to spend 5 minutes deleting photos every Sunday.

**Enable iCloud Photos optimization.** Settings → Photos → Optimize iPhone Storage. This keeps full-resolution photos in iCloud and smaller versions on your phone.

**Turn off auto-save for apps.** WhatsApp, Instagram, and other apps often save photos to your camera roll automatically. Disable this in each app's settings.

**Use "Hide" instead of keeping.** For photos you don't want to delete but don't need to see (receipts, documents), use the Hide feature. They stay in your library but don't clutter your main view.
